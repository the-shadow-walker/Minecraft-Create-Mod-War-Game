# War System (Extractors, Processing, and Territory)

## 1. Team Setup and Territory
- Each team is assigned a starting territory (Blue, Red, Green, etc.)
- Inside each territory, teams have:
  - 2 extractor plant slots (pre-defined build zones)
  - 1 main factory location (pre-built base structure area)

- The factory location is the only place where:
  - Scrap processing occurs
  - Furnace upgrades are applied
  - Core industrial production happens

---

## 2. Resource System (Scrap Extraction)

### Base Extraction
- Each extractor produces:
  - 1 scrap per hour
- Extraction requires manual interaction by default (Create-style cranking / mechanical input)

### Automation and Upgrades

#### A. Mechanical Upgrades (Create-based)
- Increasing RPM / torque improves output
- Can be upgraded up to a maximum of 4x total efficiency
- Includes combined improvements from:
  - Mechanical speed (Create power systems)
  - Infrastructure upgrades

#### B. Netherite Upgrade System (placeholder premium resource)
- Netherite ingot can be inserted into an extractor to boost speed:
  - First upgrade: +50%
  - Second upgrade: +30%
  - Third upgrade: +20%
  - All upgrades after that (up to 10 total): +10% each

- Netherite upgrades stack with mechanical upgrades
- Netherite upgrades strongly improve efficiency but are not required

---

## 3. Map-Based Extraction Points

- 15 neutral extraction points exist across the map
- These can be captured by any team
- Function like standard extractors once captured
- Serve as major conflict zones

---

## 4. Carrying and Logistics System

- Players can only carry 1 scrap at a time
- This enforces:
  - Transport infrastructure
  - Trains, vehicles, supply lines
  - Team logistics gameplay

---

## 5. Factory and Processing System

### Factory Role
- Scrap is processed only at the factory location

### Base Processing Rate
- 1 scrap takes 2 hours to process
- Fully AFK once loaded

### Automation Limits
- Auto-loaders can be built
- Only 1 scrap can be loaded at a time per system

### Factory Upgrades
- Processing systems can be upgraded using:
  - Create mechanical power (RPM/torque)
  - Netherite upgrades (efficiency boosts)

- No hard cap on factory scaling

---

## 6. Territory Capture System (Extractors)

### Weekday Capture Window
- 9 PM to 10 PM (weekdays only)
- Extractors can be contested during this time

### Capture Method
- Player must:
  - Reach extractor core
  - Change flag to their team color

### Capture Locking
- Must be held for 5 minutes to lock ownership

### Notifications
- Server announces ownership changes

### Combat Rules
- Full combat allowed:
  - Artillery
  - Aircraft
  - Base defenses

---

## 7. War Time System

- During war time:
  - Any extractor can be attacked at any time
  - Capture requires holding until Sunday 10 PM to finalize ownership

---

## 8. Extractor Ownership Stability Rules

- After capture:
  - Extractors are locked for 1 week
- Applies only to first 5 extractors per team
- Additional extractors remain fully contestable

---

## 9. Factory Destruction and Respawn Rules

### Furnace Destruction During War

- Furnace respawns next Friday at 6 PM
- Respawns at original factory location

---

### Resource Rewards

#### Manual raid (player break):
- Attacker receives 100% of weekly production

#### Bombing/artillery destruction:
- Attacker receives 50% of weekly production

---

### Upgrade Preservation
- Netherite upgrades remain after destruction

---

### Bombing Penalty
- Factory output reduced to 50% for that week

---

## 10. Surrender System

### Resource Transfer
- Winning team receives:
  - 60% of surrendered team’s previous week output
  - Lasts for 2 weeks

### Surrendering Team Penalty
- 40% efficiency for 3 days after surrender

### Anti-Exploitation Rule
- All calculations use previous week production only

---

## 11. Core Design Philosophy

- Territory and extractors drive conflict
- Logistics matter due to limited carrying capacity
- Factories are strategic industrial targets
- Combat focuses on infrastructure disruption
- Progression comes from engineering, not raw expansion
